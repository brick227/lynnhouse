Victim Advocate

Support survivors with safety planning, advocacy, referrals, and compassionate, trauma-informed guidance.

Minimal Qualifications

Minimal: Education

  • High school diploma or GED (minimum)
  • Associate’s degree in human services, criminal justice, psychology, sociology, or related field (recommended)

Minimal: Certifications

  • Domestic Violence / Sexual Assault Advocacy training (state-approved or equivalent)
  • Confidentiality & ethics training (HIPAA/advocacy standards as applicable)

Minimal: Experience

  • 0–2 years in advocacy, case management, shelter, or related human services
  • Ability to work rotating/on-call shifts (as needed)

Minimal: Skills

  • Trauma-informed communication and crisis response
  • Accurate documentation and confidentiality
  • Cultural humility and non-judgmental support

Preferred Qualifications

Preferred: Education

  • Bachelor’s degree in social work, sociology, psychology, criminal justice, or related field

Preferred: Certifications

  • Victim advocacy certification (where available)
  • Mental Health First Aid
  • CPR/First Aid

Preferred: Experience

  • 2–4 years in victim services, shelter advocacy, or community case management

Preferred: Skills

  • Court accompaniment and protective order support experience
  • Community resource navigation and referral coordination
